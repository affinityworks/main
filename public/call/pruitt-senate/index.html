This is an all-hands-on-deck moment.

Thursday afternoon, an Oklahoma judge ruled that Scott Pruitt, Donald Trump’s nominee for EPA Administrator, must release up to 3,000 emails containing his communications with coal, oil, and gas companies by next Tuesday. Under Pruitt’s direction, the Oklahoma Attorney General’s office has tried to keep the emails secret for over two years.

It is essential that the Senate -- and the public -- have a chance to examine those emails before the Senate votes on Pruitt’s confirmation.

But Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell is forcing the Senate to vote Friday even though his emails won’t be released until next Tuesday. As Senator Jeff Merkley of Oregon has said: “This is an egregious cover up that must not stand.”
